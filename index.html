Welcome to my website!

Hi, My name is Harshavardhan BV. I'm a PhD student in the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore. I am working with Dr. Mohit Kumar Jolly and Dr. Srimonta Gayen in studying heterogeneity in cancer. I am also a receipent of the prestigios Prime Minister's Research Fellowship. I have completed my bachelors and masters in science from the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Pune.

I do sudo-biology. That means I don't work with biological specimens like a stereotypical (true) biologist. Instead, I'm more interested in the applications of computational and mathematical techniques in solving complex problems in biology. However, I am also interested in general computing and like to do coding, digital design among other things. I'm an aficionado of FOSS and open-access research.

Research Experience

Deciphering the landscape of phenotypic heterogeneity in Glioblastoma

July 2023 - November 2023
Under Dr. Mohit Jolly

Analysed publicly available RNASeq datasets to discerning if the subtypes defined for GBM in previous works are truly distinct. Found that not all states are antagonistic and the Proneural/Neural-progenitor - Mesenchymal states to be the most antagonistic pair.

Mathematical modelling of X reactivation dynamics

December 2022 - March 2023
Under Dr. Mohit Jolly and Dr. Srimonta Gayen, IISc Bengaluru

Developed a phenomenological mathematical model that attempts to reproduce the dynamics of X-reactivation and erasure of upregulation. The case where both the chromosomes inhibit each other and self-inhibit is able to best explain the observed behaviour.
Preprint available at: 10.1101/2020.12.23.424181

Implications of resource competition for adaptive therapy of prostate cancer

August 2020 - July 2021
Under Prof. Sutirth Dey, IISER Pune

Developed an ODE based model which simulates the different cells types and resources in the microenvironment. The effect of the level of resources dependence on competition between cells was studied. And this was used to find levels where they would be beneficial for success of adaptive therapy using abiraterone.
Preprint available at: 10.1101/2023.09.19.558379

Investigating Cancer Cell Populations using Agent-based Simulations

August 2019 - April 2019
Under Prof. Sutirth Dey, IISER Pune

Modelled the system of Castration-Resistant Prostate Cancer as three types of cells in PhysiCell along with resources in microenvironment. It was observed that the competitive outcomes depended on the spatial distribution to some extent.

Mathematical Models of Somatic Evolution of Cancer

June 2019 - July 2019
Under Prof. Sutirth Dey, IISER Pune

The project involved reading and trying out the different methods of modelling a system of cancer cells. Ran into a bug in a agent-based modelling framework, PhysiCell, and successfully debugged in collaboration with the maintainers.

Diffusion in Nano-confined Water

May 2018 - July 2018
Under Dr. Shivprasad Patil, IISER Pune

Learnt to operate a novel Near-field Scanning Optical Microscope (NSOM) + Atomic Force Microscope (AFM) that would confine water at nano-meter scale as well as to perform Fluorescence Correlation Spectroscopy (FCS) on unconfined water to measure diffusion. The existing control software led to crashing and damaging the tips, so helped with rewriting the logic of the software in LabVIEW.
