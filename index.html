Hi, My name is Harshavardhan BV. I'm a PhD student in the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore. I am working with Dr. Mohit Kumar Jolly and Dr. Srimonta Gayen in studying allelic heterogeneity in cancer. I have completed my bachelors and masters in science from the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Pune.

I do sudo-biology. That means I don't work with biological specimens like a stereotypical (true) biologist. Instead, I'm more interested in the applications of computational and mathematical techniques in solving complex problems in biology. My Experience

Implications of resource competition for adaptive therapy of prostate cancer

August 2020 - July 2021
Under Prof. Sutirth Dey, IISER Pune

Developed an ODE based model which simulates the different cells types and resources in the microenvironment. The effect of the level of resources dependence on competition between cells was studied. And this was used to find levels where they would be beneficial for success of adaptive therapy using abiraterone.

Investigating Cancer Cell Populations using Agent-based Simulations

August 2019 - April 2019
Under Prof. Sutirth Dey, IISER Pune

Modelled the system of Castration-Resistant Prostate Cancer as three types of cells in the agent-based framework of PhysiCell along with resources in microenvironment. The model was calibrated to scaled cell line data and competition was simulated between each pair. It was observed that the competitive outcomes depended on the spatial distribution to some extent.

Mathematical Models of Somatic Evolution of Cancer

June 2019 - July 2019
Under Prof. Sutirth Dey, IISER Pune

The project involved reading and trying out the different methods of modelling a system of cancer cells. While trying out the sample projects of a C++ based agent-based modelling framework, PhysiCell ran into a segmentation fault. Debugged in GDB in collaboration with the maintainers and found the underlying reason for this. A temporary fix was proposed, but a more permanent solution would be taken care by the maintainers.

Diffusion in Nano-confined Water

May 2018 - July 2018
Under Dr. Shivprasad Patil, IISER Pune

Learnt to operate a novel Near-field Scanning Optical Microscope (NSOM) + Atomic Force Microscope (AFM) that would confine water at nano-meter scale as well as to perform Fluorescence Correlation Spectroscopy (FCS) on unconfined water to measure diffusion. The existing control software led to crashing and damaging the tips, so helped with rewriting the logic of the software in LabVIEW.
